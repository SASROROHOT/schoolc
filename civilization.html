Geography & Climate

The UK is located in north-western Europe, bordered by the English Channel to the south, the North Sea to the east, and the Irish Sea and the Atlantic Ocean to the west. It includes Great Britain, Northern Ireland, thousands of smaller islands, the Channel Islands, and the Isle of Man, plus overseas territories (e.g. Bermuda, Cayman Islands). The climate is a temperate maritime climate ‚Äî temperatures rarely drop below -11¬∞C or rise above +35¬∞C, with plentiful rainfall all year round.

Economy

The UK has the world's fifth largest economy. Brexit ‚Äî the UK's decision to leave the EU following a June 2016 referendum (51.89% voted to leave) ‚Äî has had a significant impact. The UK formally left the EU in March 2019.

Primary sector: About 2% of the workforce. Key crops: wheat, barley, oats, sugar beets, potatoes, rapeseed. Livestock: cattle, sheep, pigs and poultry.

Secondary sector: Declining since the 1970s when manufacturing contributed up to 25% of GDP. Today it generates employment for 18.36% of the workforce and ranks the UK as the 11th manufacturing nation. Key industries: automotive (Aston Martin, Bentley, Jaguar, Land Rover, Rolls-Royce), aerospace, pharmaceuticals, food & drink, tobacco, textiles, fashion, printing & publishing.

Tertiary sector: Employs over 82% of the workforce and contributes 70% of GDP. London is the financial hub since the Middle Ages ‚Äî banking, insurance, forex, and bonds. Tourism: 42 million visitors in 2017. Transport: 49,904 km of roads, 3,497 km of motorways, 213,750 km of paved roads. Railways cover over 17,000 km (34% electrified). 40+ commercial airports.

The Four Sectors of the Economy

A country's economy is divided into: Primary sector (raw materials: mining, farming, fishing), Secondary sector (manufacturing: cars, steel), Tertiary sector (services: banking, insurance, restaurants, transport, education), and Quaternary sector (the R&D part of the tertiary sector: IT).

Geography & Climate

Ireland occupies five-sixths of the westernmost major island of the British Isles, separated from Great Britain by the Irish Sea. It is divided into 26 counties (the other 6 make up Northern Ireland). The island's main features include low central plains surrounded by coastal mountains. The highest peak is Carrauntoohil (Corran Tuathail in Irish), at 1,041 metres. The island is bisected by the river Shannon (360.5 km / 224 miles), Ireland's longest river.

Ireland is known as the "Emerald Isle" because its mild temperatures and plentiful rainfall create perfect conditions for lush, green landscapes. The southwest coast is particularly mild in winter. The largest lake is Lough Corrib.

History & Emigration

No country in Europe has been as affected by emigration as Ireland. The Great Famine of 1845‚Äì1855 caused by a blight on potato crops left millions without food. Emigration peaked then, with nearly as many people living outside Ireland as within it ‚Äî mostly to North America. Ireland remained the poor man of north-west Europe for most of the 20th century.

The Celtic Tiger

In the 1990s, the Irish economy exploded into life. Employment almost doubled between 1993 and 2007. Strong economic growth led Ireland to create a single market in Europe, attracting foreign companies with favourable taxation and a highly educated workforce. Ireland became the so-called "Celtic Tiger." However, the global recession hit in 2008, and Ireland had to request financial assistance from the European Commission, the ECB and the IMF.

Economy Today

Ireland's economy has recovered strongly. It is a knowledge economy focused on services like high-tech, life sciences, and financial services. Main trading partners: USA, UK, Germany, France, Spain, Netherlands. Key products: organic chemicals, medical & pharmaceutical products, computers.

Agriculture: 2% of Irish GDP. 5 million of 7 million hectares are dedicated to agriculture. Main products: beef, dairy, barley, potatoes, wheat, mushrooms. The fishing industry employs over 11,000 people.

Tourism: Over 5 million visitors in 2018. Built around the Irish people, culture, and the physical beauty of the landscape.

Geography & Climate

Located in the western hemisphere in North America, the US consists of 50 states (48 conterminous + Alaska and Hawaii) and a federal district (Washington D.C.). The conterminous states stretch more than 4,500 km from east to west and more than 2,500 km from north to south. Bordered by Canada to the north, the Atlantic Ocean to the east, the Gulf of Mexico and Mexico to the south, and the Pacific Ocean to the west. It has a continental climate ‚Äî cold winters and hot summers ‚Äî though varied by latitude and distance from the sea.

The US flag (Stars and Stripes) consists of a blue rectangle in the canton representing the 50 states, and 13 horizontal red-and-white stripes representing the 13 British colonies that declared independence.

Economy

The US is the world's third-largest economy and operates as a mixed economy ‚Äî a free market economy in consumer goods and business services, but with government regulation to protect the public good.

Agriculture: 827,000+ people employed. Farms decreased from 2.16 million in 2000 to 2.06 million in 2017. Particularly concentrated in the Great Plains and the Corn Belt around the Great Lakes. Main crops: corn, wheat, soybeans, tobacco. Most common farm animals: cattle, pigs, hens. California produces a majority of fruits, vegetables, and nuts.

Manufacturing: Central to the economy. US factories produce twice as many goods as in 1984 with one-third fewer workers. Top manufactured products: refined petroleum products (petrol, jet fuel, liquefied refinery gases), pharmaceuticals, airplanes and automobiles, iron and steel. Technology & new work organization has revolutionized the factory since the 1980s.

Consumer Goods: Appliances, tyres, furniture, gifts, school and office products, jewellery, and sporting goods ‚Äî sold at mono/multi-brand stores or large retail shops. Top global brands: Apple, Google, IBM, McDonald's, Microsoft, Amazon.

Tourism: Accounts for 8.8% of GDP, employing 7.9 million people. Top destinations: New York, San Francisco, Washington, Philadelphia, Las Vegas, Miami. Natural wonders include national parks, the beaches of California and Florida, the Mississippi and its tributaries, and the Walt Disney theme parks.

Government Structure

The US has a constitution-based federal republic with three branches: the executive (President), the legislative (Congress: Senate + House of Representatives), and the judiciary (Supreme Court).
